Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

A very basic but tasty recipe for spaghetti carbonara. Enough for 2 hungry adults.

It's flavor stands or falls with the quality of it's ingredients!

Ingredients

  • 4 eggs
  • 500 g spaghetti
  • 100 g bacon (Ideally streaky bacon)
  • 200 g Pecorino (or any parmesan)
  • Salt, pepper, oregano

Tools

  • Large frying pan with lid
  • Medium/Large pot
  • Medium/Large pasta strainer
  • Some small/medium bowls

Steps

  1. Cut the bacon into small cubes. Grate the parmesan, preferably finely. Crack the eggs, but put egg white and york into different bowls.
    You can put 1-2 tablespoons of egg white in the bowl with the york.
    You will not need the rest of the egg white for this recipe.

  2. Fill the pot with water and a great bit of salt. Heat until it boils.
    While you wait, put the bacon and a small splash of water into the frying pan and heat on low - medium temperature.
    Put a lid on top of the pan.

  3. Put the parmesan into the bowl with egg york. Whisk until a continuous paste is created.
    If it does not result in a paste, you can:

    • Add another egg york, if too firm
    • Grate more parmesan into it, if too liquid

  4. As the pasta becomes al dente, put it into the pasta strainer. If possible, keep some hot pasta water.
    Turn down the heat for pot and pan and add the pasta to the bacon. Whisk a bit.
    As the temperature of the pan went from medium to low heat, add the egg york and parmesan mixture to the pasta.
    DON'T add the mixture to the pasta while the pan is still at medium heat. The egg would curdle!
    Now whisk quickly. Don't keep it on the plate for too long.

  5. You are done. Put it on plates and enjoy.
    Be aware: Heating this dish in a microwave might make the egg curdle!
